Description

Object detection in Earth Vision, also known asEarth Observation and Remote Sensing, refers to the problem of localizing objects of interest (e.g., vehicles and airplanes) on the earthâ€™s surface and predicting their corresponding land-use categories. Observing plenty of instances from the overhead view provide a new way to understand the world. This is a relatively new field, with many new applications waiting to be developed. For movable categories, such as vehicles, ships,and planes, the orientation estimation is important for tracking.The majority of computer vision research focuses mostly on images from everyday life. However,the aerial imagery is a rich and structured source of information, yet, it is less investigated. The task of object detection in aerial images is distinguished from the conventional object detectiontask in the following respects:

  • The scale variations of object instances in aerial images are considerably huge.
  • Many small object instances are densely distributed in aerial images, for example, the ships in a harbor and the vehicles in a parking lot.
  • Objects in aerial images often appear in arbitrary orientations.

This contest, organizing on CVPR'2019, features a new large-scale image database of object detection in aerial images.

Through the dataset and the tasks, we aim to draw attention from the a wide range of communities and call for more future research and efforts on the problems of object dection in aerial images.
